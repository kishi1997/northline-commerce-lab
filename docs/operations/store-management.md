# Store Management Runbook / 店舗運用手順

All instructions below apply to disposable local or Playground demo data. Never enter real customer or payment details.

## Product content

1. Open **Products → All Products** and choose a product.
2. Update the title, long/short description, category, regular/sale price, SKU, and linked products.
3. For variable products, use **Attributes** then **Variations**; confirm each variation has a price and stock state.
4. Preview before publishing and verify the product at desktop and mobile widths.

## Product images and gallery

Seeded imagery is generated by `npm run assets:build`; source and license ownership remain in the repository. To test a manual replacement:

1. Open a product and select **Set product image** or **Add product gallery images**.
2. Upload an optimized WebP with descriptive alternative text.
3. Avoid text baked into the image when the same information can be HTML.
4. Confirm crop behavior in shop, category, product, cart, and checkout contexts.

Do not add remote image URLs, real brands, stock subscriptions, or unclear licenses. Record every permanent asset in `docs/asset-register.md`.

## Homepage banner and category route

1. Open **Pages → Home → Edit**.
2. Select the hero or editorial banner inside the Northline homepage pattern.
3. Keep one H1, preserve the demo disclaimer, and keep the primary CTA linked to `/shop/`.
4. Use copper only for actions/status; verify contrast and mobile document order.
5. Categories are populated from WooCommerce data; update category title/thumbnail under **Products → Categories**.

## Inventory

1. Open a product and locate **Product data → Inventory**.
2. Preserve a unique SKU, enable stock management, and change quantity.
3. Confirm these seeded states remain represented: normal, low stock (2 units), and out of stock (0 units).
4. Verify the shop label and add-to-cart availability. No real low-stock email is delivered.

## Coupon

1. Open **Marketing → Coupons**.
2. Duplicate or edit `NORTHLINE10`.
3. Set type, amount, expiry, minimum spend, product/category restrictions, and usage limit.
4. Test valid, invalid, expired, and minimum-spend states in Cart/Checkout Block.

## Restricted-product rules

1. Open a product and use **Product data → General**.
2. Set **Restricted product**, a plain-text notice (maximum 240 characters), and comma-separated Canadian codes such as `BC, AB, ON`.
3. Save and verify the product notice and cart item data.
4. Test an allowed and denied destination. UI text alone is never considered enforcement; the server response is authoritative.

## Order handling

1. Place a test order using **Test order — no payment** and fictional `example.com` data.
2. Open **WooCommerce → Orders** and confirm item metadata contains the purchase notice, allowed regions at purchase, and identity status.
3. Move the order through Processing, On hold, Completed, or Cancelled to demonstrate workflow ownership.
4. Confirm customer order details show the same public item metadata. Email templates inherit it, but delivery is disabled.

## Cancellation and refund

1. Open the order and select **Refund**.
2. Enter line quantity/amount and a fictional reason.
3. Choose **Refund manually**; never invoke a gateway.
4. Confirm refunded totals and status. No funds move because the configured payment method captures nothing.
5. For cancellation, add an internal note and change status to Cancelled; verify stock restoration behavior.

## 日本語要約

商品、画像、バナー、在庫、クーポン、地域制限、注文、手動返金を管理画面から更新する。公開デモでは架空情報だけを使用し、実メール・実決済・実ブランド素材を使わない。地域制限は表示確認だけでなく必ずサーバー側の成功・失敗を確認する。
